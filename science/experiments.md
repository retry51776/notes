## Behavioral Ecology

### Universe 25 (Mouse Utopia)

`Universe 25` was John B. Calhoun's famous rodent population experiment.

- Setup:
  - Mice were given abundant food, water, shelter, and no predators.
  - The enclosure removed most external survival pressure.
- Main observation:
  - Population initially grew quickly, then social behavior degraded.
  - Territorial stress, social withdrawal, and abnormal behavior increased.
  - Birth rates collapsed even before resources were fully exhausted.
- Why people still discuss it:
  - It is often used to think about density, social structure, and behavioral collapse.
  - It is not a direct one-to-one model for human society, but it is a useful cautionary case.

## Closed Ecosystems

### Biosphere 2, Arizona (1991)

`Biosphere 2` in Oracle, Arizona, was a large closed ecological system experiment.

- Key date:
  - First sealed mission started on **September 26, 1991**.
- Goal:
  - Test whether humans and multiple biomes could survive in a materially closed system.
- Main outcomes:
  - Oxygen levels dropped significantly and required outside intervention.
  - Food production and ecological balance were harder than expected.
  - The experiment produced valuable data for life-support systems and systems ecology.
- Why it matters:
  - It revealed how difficult closed-loop environmental control is.
  - It informed thinking about sustainability and future space habitats.

## IMO

- High-level lesson:
  - "Enough resources" is not sufficient by itself.
  - Stable systems also need healthy feedback loops and social/ecological balance.
